## TASK A COMPANIES

### 1. TATA MOTORS (Private Sector)

**Overview:**
- Established 1945 as Tata Engineering and Locomotive Co. Ltd. (TELCO)
- Headquartered in Mumbai, India
- Second-largest carmaker in India
- Listed on BSE and NSE (SENSEX and NIFTY 50)

**Ownership Structure:**
- Owned by Tata Group (conglomerate)
- Tata Sons is the holding company
- Majority of Tata Sons' equity held by philanthropic trusts
- 58,442 employees as of December 2024

**Key Features:**
- Public limited company (PLC)
- Profit-oriented
- Shareholders can buy/sell shares
- Board of directors makes strategic decisions
- Major subsidiaries: Jaguar Land Rover, Tata Technologies

**Recent Developments (2024):**
- Split into two entities: Commercial vehicles + Passenger vehicles
- Top EV seller in India (61,496 units in 2024)

**Source:** Wikipedia, Tracxn, MarketScreener

### 2. NHS SCOTLAND (Public Sector)

**Overview:**
- Publicly-funded healthcare system
- Created by National Health Service (Scotland) Act 1947
- Came into effect 5 July 1948
- One of four NHS systems in the UK

**Organizational Structure:**
- 14 territorial NHS Boards
- 7 Special NHS Boards
- 1 Public Health Scotland body
- Each Board has governance accountable to Scottish Ministers

**Key Features:**
- Funded by taxation (not profit-driven)
- Services are FREE at point of delivery
- Comprehensive and universal coverage
- Accountable to government, not shareholders

**Workforce & Resources:**
- Approximately 160,000 staff
- £17 billion annual expenditure
- 31 Integration Joint Boards (IJBs)

**Objectives (HEAT Targets):**
- Health Improvement
- Efficiency
- Access
- Treatment quality

**Source:** NHS Scotland, gov.scot, Wikipedia

### 3. OXFAM (Third Sector / Charity)

**Overview:**
- International NGO focused on ending poverty
- Founded 1942 in Oxford as "Oxford Committee for Famine Relief"
- Operates in 90+ countries

**Activities:**
- Humanitarian response (30+ countries)
- Long-term development partnerships
- Advocacy for policy change

## TASK B COMPANIES

### TOYOTA - Positive Internal Factors

**The Toyota Way (2 Pillars):**
1. Respect for People
2. Continuous Improvement (Kaizen)

**Key Success Factors:**

1. **Corporate Culture:**
   - "Kaizen mind" - never-ending drive to improve
   - "Genchi genbutsu" - solve problems at source
   - "Mutual ownership of problems"

2. **Toyota Production System (TPS):**
   - Jidoka - automation with human touch (machines stop if problem detected)
   - Just-in-Time (JIT) - produce only what's needed, when needed
   - Eliminates waste, reduces inventory costs

3. **Quality Management:**
   - Quality built into every process
   - Continuous improvement culture
   - Employee empowerment - workers can stop production line

4. **Teamwork:**
   - All employees go through team-building training
   - Collaboration leads to greater success

**Result:** One of world's most successful automakers for 70+ years

**Source:** Wikipedia, Panmore, ResearchGate

---

### BOEING - Negative Internal Factors

**The 737 MAX Crisis:**
- Two fatal crashes (Lion Air 2018, Ethiopian Airlines 2019)
- 346 lives lost
- Global fleet grounding
- $2.5 billion settlement with US government

**Key Internal Failures:**

1. **Culture Shift (Profit over Safety):**
   - Former CEOs changed culture from "engineering excellence" to "run like a business"
   - CEO Stonecipher: "intent was to change Boeing's culture so it is run like a business rather than a great engineering firm"
   - Safety subordinated to shareholder value

2. **Management Disconnect:**
   - Senior management disconnected from workers
   - Inadequate safety culture implementation
   - Concerns dismissed or ignored

3. **Whistleblower Suppression:**
   - Engineers knew about MCAS problems before crashes
   - Information not shared with FAA, airlines, or pilots
   - Senior engineer: "management was more concerned with cost and schedule than safety"

4. **Governance Failures:**
   - Audit committee focused only on financial risks
   - After Lion Air crash, "product safety" not mentioned as compliance risk
   - Board failed oversight duties

**Financial Impact:** $87 billion lost in shareholder value since 2018

**Source:** Harvard Business School, MIT Sloan, CPA Journal

### NETFLIX - PESTEC Analysis

**Political:**
- Content censorship varies by country (removed Patriot Act episode in Saudi Arabia)
- Digital taxes in Europe (France, Italy) increase costs
- Must navigate different regulations in 190+ countries

**Economic:**
- High inflation affects consumer spending on subscriptions
- Currency fluctuations impact revenue (operates globally)
- Counter-cyclical: affordable entertainment during recessions

**Social:**
- Shift to on-demand, binge-watching culture (Netflix pioneered this)
- Growing demand for diverse, local content
- Competition from short-form (TikTok) affecting viewing habits

**Technological:**
- AI-powered recommendation engine (key competitive advantage)
- Own CDN (Open Connect) for better streaming
- 4K, HDR, potential VR content

**Environmental:**
- Data centers are energy-intensive
- Working on energy efficiency
- Invested in renewable energy (wind power)

**Competitive:**
- Intense competition: Disney+, Amazon Prime, Apple TV+
- Password sharing crackdown
- Ad-supported tier introduced

**Source:** Panmore, MBA Skool, Business Model Analyst

## STAKEHOLDER CONFLICTS (B3)

**Conflict 1: Shareholders vs Employees**
- Shareholders want maximum dividends → pressure to cut costs
- Employees want higher wages, job security
- Example: Boeing prioritized shareholder returns over employee safety concerns

**Conflict 2: Customers vs Environment**
- Customers want cheap products
- Environmental groups want sustainable practices (often more expensive)
- Example: McDonald's balancing cheap food with sustainable packaging goals

**Conflict 3: Local Community vs Company Expansion**
- Company wants to expand operations
- Local community concerned about traffic, pollution, character of area
- Example: Protests against new factory locations
