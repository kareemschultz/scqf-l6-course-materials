  SME DEFINITION GUIDE - KEY FACTS AND CRITERIA
================================================================================

  This guide provides factual reference information about SME definitions.
  Use it to understand the criteria, then write your own explanation in
  your report using your own words and your own chosen examples.

================================================================================
  WHAT DOES SME STAND FOR?
================================================================================

  SME = Small and Medium-sized Enterprise

  These are businesses that fall below certain thresholds for number of
  employees, annual turnover, and/or balance sheet total.

================================================================================
  EU / UK DEFINITION (Most commonly used in SQA assessments)
================================================================================

  Category        | Employees  | Annual Turnover    | Balance Sheet Total
  ────────────────|────────────|────────────────────|────────────────────
  Micro           | < 10       | Up to 2 million    | Up to 2 million
  Small           | < 50       | Up to 10 million   | Up to 10 million
  Medium          | < 250      | Up to 50 million   | Up to 43 million

  To qualify as an SME, a business must meet the employee threshold AND
  at least one of the two financial thresholds.

  Note: Figures above are in Euros (EU definition). UK equivalents are
  broadly similar.

================================================================================
  OTHER DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

  OECD Definition:
  - Generally defines SMEs as firms with fewer than 250 employees.
  - Notes that definitions vary by country.

  UK Companies Act 2006:
  - Small company: turnover up to 10.2 million GBP, balance sheet up to
    5.1 million GBP, up to 50 employees (meet 2 of 3).
  - Medium company: turnover up to 36 million GBP, balance sheet up to
    18 million GBP, up to 250 employees (meet 2 of 3).

  US Small Business Administration (SBA):
  - Definitions vary by industry sector.
  - Generally: fewer than 500 employees for manufacturing, under certain
    revenue thresholds for services.

================================================================================
  WHY SMEs MATTER
================================================================================

  Key statistics to be aware of (check for current figures when writing):

  - SMEs make up approximately 99% of all businesses in the UK.
  - They employ roughly 60% of the private sector workforce.
  - They generate around 50% of private sector turnover.
  - The vast majority of SMEs are micro-businesses (fewer than 10 employees).

  Source for current stats: Federation of Small Businesses (fsb.org.uk)
  or Office for National Statistics (ons.gov.uk)

================================================================================
  KEY CHARACTERISTICS TO EXPLORE IN YOUR REPORT
================================================================================

  Your brief asks you to discuss SME characteristics. Common characteristics
  include (choose the ones your brief specifies):

  1. Ownership and control
     - Often owned and managed by the same person or family
     - Flatter management structures
     - Quicker decision-making but more risk concentrated on one person

  2. Limited access to finance
     - Harder to get bank loans than large firms
     - Rely more on personal savings, family funds, or small grants
     - May struggle to grow without external investment

  3. Flexibility and adaptability
     - Can respond to market changes faster than large organisations
     - Less bureaucracy and shorter communication chains
     - Can pivot business models more easily

  4. Local or niche market focus
     - Many SMEs serve local communities or specialist markets
     - Can build strong customer relationships
     - Vulnerable to local economic downturns

  5. Limited resources
     - Fewer staff, smaller budgets, less specialist expertise
     - Owners often perform multiple roles
     - Training and development may be informal

  NOTE: You must apply these characteristics to a specific SECTOR that
  your brief requires (e.g. hospitality, retail, technology, etc.).
  Do not just list characteristics in the abstract.
