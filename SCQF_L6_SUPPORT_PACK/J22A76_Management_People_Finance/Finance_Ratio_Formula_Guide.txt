================================================================================
  ACCOUNTING RATIOS - FORMULA REFERENCE GUIDE
================================================================================

  This guide lists common accounting ratios, their formulas, and what each
  one measures. Use this as a reference when writing your finance section.
  You must explain each ratio IN YOUR OWN WORDS in your report.

================================================================================
  PROFITABILITY RATIOS
================================================================================

  1. GROSS PROFIT RATIO (Gross Profit Margin)
     ─────────────────────────────────────────
     Formula:  (Gross Profit / Revenue) x 100

     Measures: The percentage of revenue remaining after deducting the
     cost of goods sold. Shows how efficiently a business converts
     sales into gross profit.

     Example calculation:
       Revenue = 500,000
       Cost of Sales = 300,000
       Gross Profit = 200,000
       Ratio = (200,000 / 500,000) x 100 = 40%


  2. NET PROFIT RATIO (Net Profit Margin)
     ────────────────────────────────────
     Formula:  (Net Profit / Revenue) x 100

     Measures: The percentage of revenue remaining after ALL expenses
     (including overheads, interest, tax). Shows overall profitability.

     Example calculation:
       Revenue = 500,000
       Net Profit = 75,000
       Ratio = (75,000 / 500,000) x 100 = 15%


  3. RETURN ON CAPITAL EMPLOYED (ROCE)
     ──────────────────────────────────
     Formula:  (Operating Profit / Capital Employed) x 100

     Where Capital Employed = Total Assets - Current Liabilities

     Measures: How effectively the business uses its total capital
     (both equity and long-term debt) to generate profit.

================================================================================
  LIQUIDITY RATIOS
================================================================================

  4. CURRENT RATIO
     ─────────────
     Formula:  Current Assets / Current Liabilities

     Measures: Whether the business can pay its short-term debts with
     its short-term assets. A ratio of 1.5:1 to 2:1 is generally
     considered healthy.

     Example calculation:
       Current Assets = 120,000
       Current Liabilities = 80,000
       Ratio = 120,000 / 80,000 = 1.5:1


  5. ACID TEST RATIO (Quick Ratio)
     ─────────────────────────────
     Formula:  (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities

     Measures: Same as current ratio but EXCLUDES inventory (stock),
     because inventory cannot always be sold quickly. More conservative
     measure of short-term liquidity.

     Example calculation:
       Current Assets = 120,000
       Inventory = 40,000
       Current Liabilities = 80,000
       Ratio = (120,000 - 40,000) / 80,000 = 1:1

================================================================================
  EFFICIENCY RATIOS
================================================================================

  6. INVENTORY TURNOVER
     ──────────────────
     Formula:  Cost of Sales / Average Inventory

     Or expressed in days:
     (Average Inventory / Cost of Sales) x 365

     Measures: How many times the business sells and replaces its
     stock in a year. Higher turnover = faster-moving stock.


  7. TRADE RECEIVABLES DAYS (Debtor Days)
     ─────────────────────────────────────
     Formula:  (Trade Receivables / Revenue) x 365

     Measures: How many days on average it takes customers to pay.
     Lower = better cash flow.


  8. TRADE PAYABLES DAYS (Creditor Days)
     ────────────────────────────────────
     Formula:  (Trade Payables / Cost of Sales) x 365

     Measures: How many days on average the business takes to pay
     its suppliers.

================================================================================
  HOW TO USE RATIOS IN YOUR REPORT
================================================================================

  For each ratio you discuss:
  1. State the formula clearly
  2. Explain what the ratio measures in plain language
  3. Give a worked example with numbers (you can make up simple figures)
  4. Explain what the result means for business decision-making
  5. Mention any limitations (e.g. current ratio ignores cash timing)

  DO NOT just list formulas. The assessor wants to see that you understand
  what each ratio TELLS a business owner or manager.

================================================================================
  KEY TERMINOLOGY
================================================================================

  Revenue / Turnover / Sales    = Money received from selling goods/services
  Cost of Sales / Cost of Goods = Direct costs of producing what was sold
  Gross Profit                  = Revenue minus Cost of Sales
  Net Profit                    = Revenue minus ALL costs
  Operating Profit              = Profit before interest and tax
  Current Assets                = Cash, stock, debtors (receivable within 1 year)
  Current Liabilities           = Debts due within 1 year
  Capital Employed              = Total assets minus current liabilities
  Trade Receivables             = Money owed TO the business by customers
  Trade Payables                = Money owed BY the business to suppliers
